What is BMI?

BMI, or Body Mass Index, is a tool that healthcare providers use to estimate the amount of body fat by using your height and weight measurements. It can help assess risk factors for certain health conditions. The BMI is not always an accurate representation of body fatness.

BMI Categories

The following are the standard BMI categories:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI between 18.5 and 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI between 25.0 and 29.9
  • Obesity: BMI of 30.0 or higher
